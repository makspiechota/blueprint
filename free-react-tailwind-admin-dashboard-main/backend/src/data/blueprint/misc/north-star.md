# Business North Star: The Software Factory

See [[strategy]] for detailed business strategy.

## The Vision: Software Factory

**Future State (3-5 years):**
Software development operates like modern manufacturing. Engineers design products and supervise production. AI agents execute the manufacturing. The result: systematic, scalable, high-quality software delivery.

**In a Software Factory:**
- **Engineers = Factory Engineers**: Design strategy, architecture, and production workflows. Supervise quality and optimize the factory line.
- **AI Agents = Manufacturing Robots**: Execute implementation, testing, deployment. Follow systematic procedures and quality standards.
- **BLUEPRINT = Factory Control System**: Provides specifications, business context, and instructions to AI agent workers. Single source of truth.
- **6 Practices = Factory Operating Procedures**: Ensure quality, safety, and systematic operation at scale.

**The Transformation:**
From ad-hoc, manual coding â†’ To systematic, AI-augmented software manufacturing

## The Problem We Solve

### Current Reality: Broken "Factories"

Post-PMF B2B SaaS companies (5-15 engineers, $1-5M ARR) face these challenges:

**1. Ad-hoc "Manufacturing"**
- No systematic production process
- Hero-driven, manual work
- Can't scale (hiring more people doesn't increase velocity proportionally)
- Can't leverage AI effectively (no systematic foundation)

**2. Fragmented Context**
- Strategy in slides, specs in Jira, docs in Notion, code in GitHub
- AI agents lack business context (don't understand what to build or why)
- Engineers disconnected from business outcomes
- Manual overhead: repeating context to every tool/person/agent

**3. No Factory Infrastructure**
- Can't automate safely (afraid of breaking things)
- Can't deploy frequently (manual, risky)
- Can't trust AI output (no validation systems)
- Can't scale engineering systematically

**Result:** Companies want AI-augmented development but lack the systematic foundation to make it work. Adding AI to ad-hoc processes creates chaos, not velocity.

## Our Solution: Build Your Software Factory

We help companies build Software Factories through three components:

### 1. Engineering Excellence (Factory Operating Procedures)
**Systematic practices that enable reliable, scalable software manufacturing**

**The 6 Practices Framework:**

**Execution Excellence (Foundation):**
- **Small-batch development**: Manufacture in small, reviewable increments
- **Test-driven development**: Validate quality systematically (tests as specifications for AI agents)
- **Feature flags**: Deploy safely, decouple deployment from release (safe experimentation)

**Strategic Excellence (Scale):**
- **Domain-Driven Design**: Clear specifications and shared language (context for AI agents)
- **Product Engineering culture**: Engineers understand business outcomes (design the right products)
- **CI/CD pipeline**: Automated quality checks and deployment (factory automation)

**Philosophy:**
- Engineering excellence is the operating system for Software Factories
- AI agents are powerful, but need systematic procedures to work reliably
- These practices were needed before AI; AI just makes them essential
- Without practices, AI creates chaos. With practices, AI enables factory-scale production.
